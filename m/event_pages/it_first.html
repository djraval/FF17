Talk is cheap, show me the code

The main motive of a program is to satisfy the intentions of the user. If you think your code can satisfy our intentions, you have entered the right place. And as someone has said “Give someone a program, frustrate him for a day. Teach someone to program; frustrate him/her for lifetime”. If you have learnt to get frustrated for a lifetime, get frustrated for a day then.

Round 1: Elimination Round (Using Pen and Paper)
Each participant has to solve a question paper having basic C or C++ or logic questions involving finding and resolving errors, finding outputs of given programs, etc. The qualifying participants will be selected based on ranking or cut-off marks. Maximum 20 participants will enter into Round 2.
Time Duration: 60 Minutes

Round 2: Moderate Programming Skills (Using Computer)
This round has a set of programming problems that the participant is expected to solve (design an algorithm and implement it in C or C++) as many as possible. Maximum 20 participants will enter into Round 2.
Time Duration: 60 Minutes

Rules:
1.The person will be free to choose their development tools (Turbo C++ in Windows or gcc/g++ in Linux).
2.Round 1 will have many questions which need to be attempted in 60 minutes. Solve as many questions as you can. Maximum of 20 teams move on to the next round.
3.For Round 2, the teams’ ranking will be decided on the basis of the no of problems correctly solved in the time limit.
4.Teams can use paper and pen to do calculations.
5.The organizing committee reserves right for any change/addition in rules.
